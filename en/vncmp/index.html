What is FIRST Tech Challenge?

FIRST® Tech Challenge is more than just building robots. Teams (up to 15 team members, grades 7-12) participate in the challenge to design, build, program, and operate robots to compete in leagues. The robot kits are reusable from year to year and can be programmed in various levels of the Java language. In addition to designing and building robots, teams also participate in fundraising, branding, communications, and community outreach to win various prizes.

Guided by coaches and mentors, students can develop STEM skills and practice mechanical design principles, while also embracing values such as hard work, creativity, and teamwork. With Gracious Professionalism®, students are encouraged to create their team's identity and become ambassadors for FIRST and STEM in their own communities.

Each season will conclude with regional tournaments and the FIRST Championship in the United States.

n the CENTERSTAGE℠ season, sponsored by Raytheon Technologies (RTX) and launched on September 9, 2023, the FIRST® Tech Challenge will showcase the power of design, creativity, and precision to create countless new experiences.

About FIRST Tech Challenge Vietnam

FIRST Tech Challenge Vietnam is the official FTC competition held in Vietnam under the license of FIRST in the United States.
FIRST Tech Challenge Vietnam aims to create a technological playground to encourage Vietnamese students' learning spirit, creativity, and social awareness. At the same time, it seeks to inspire and motivate students to study, practice, and apply new technologies, contributing to building a high-quality workforce to meet the demands of the Fourth Industrial Revolution.

FIRST Tech Challenge Vietnam is organized by the FPT Education Organization and FPT University, with the cooperation and support of the Vietnamese community of engineers and technology experts, as well as young people from the FIRST network in Vietnam and around the world.

Chance to be supported with a kit to participate FTC VN

Basically, teams can equip themselves with a kit according to FIRST standards (control board, motor, D/C servo, sensors from REV Robotics). The mechanical structure details teams can use external components or recycled items. But if the cost of participating in FIRST Tech Challenge Vietnam is a major obstacle for the team, the team can participate in the competition Vietnam Open Robotics Challenge 2023 to have the opportunity to receive a robot kit for the FIRST® Tech Challenge Vietnam competition.

Vietnam Open Robotics Challenge 2023 (VORC 2023) Is a talent competition that will be held in 4 regions of Vietnam to pick out teams to be supported with a robot kit for FIRST® Tech Challenge Vietnam. Vietnam Open Robotics Challenge 2023 will be held in 2 rounds: online training round and online challenge round nationwide or online combined with offline at two focal points Cần Thơ and Đà Nẵng.

Who can join?

Students from middle and high schools across the country (from grade 7 to grade 12, according to the 2023-2024 school year), forming a team of 3 to 15 people with 1 advisor (mentor) who is a teacher or any individual with expertise.

Team members are encouraged to contribute any skills you already have, such as programming, electronics, metal processing, graphic design, web design, oratory, filming, etc. The advisor will guide students and help you achieve skills and confidence in a safe, cohesive environment.

The competition takes place in one day of the final event and has 4 rounds of competition: Qualification Round, Elimination Round, Semi-final and Final.

- Qualification Round: 2-3 teams will be paired into 1 Alliance each match, to be specific, each match, 1 team will be paired with 1-2 other teams to become 1 Alliance randomly. Each team will have 4 - 5 matches in the Qualification Round. Teams will be randomly assigned to Qualification Matches. The schedule for these matches, including the match pairings and Alliance colors, will be announced before the competition day.

- Elimination Round: In the Elimination Matches, alliances compete in a bracket structure, with seed #1 facing seed #4 and seed #2 facing seed #3, to determine which alliances advance to the Semi-final. In the Elimination Matches, teams will receive win, loss, or tie results instead of Ranking Points. In each pair of matches, the matches will be held to determine which alliance continues to compete, the first team to win two matches, and the tied matches will be replayed until one alliance wins two matches. The number of teams in the Elimination Round will depend on the number of teams in the competition. If there are 21 or more teams in the competition, the Elimination Matches will have 3 teams/alliance. If there are 20 teams or fewer, each alliance will have 2 teams.

- Semi final: In the Semi-final, the seed #1 alliance will face the seed #4 alliance and the seed #2 alliance will face the seed #3 alliance. The winners of each Semi-final match will advance to the Final.

- Grand final: The 2 winning alliances in the Semi-final will continue to compete for the championship.

The youth are shaping the future

Kids have a natural curiosity, creativity, and desire to explore. At FIRST®, we understand the power of STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) to inspire their innovative spirit and boost self-esteem.

Project-based, hands-on FIRST programs introduce students to engineering and coding in an engaging, inclusive, and creative learning environment in schools and local communities. Participants work collaboratively to solve an annual, themed robotics challenge..

FIRST isn't just about robots. The team also designs educational programs from kindergarten to high school to help children develop problem-solving skills, leadership, and communication. With the support of mentors, teachers, volunteers, sponsors, parents, and alumni spread across more than 100 countries, the FIRST experience will inspire and empower students to build a bright future for themselves and the community.

Lights, Camera, STEAM!

Science, technology, engineering, arts, and math (STEAM) are the inspiration for big ideas, for boldness and creativity. With these skills, we can create art and experiences to connect, bring joy and emotions to each other.

In the 2023-2024 season, the FIRST® IN SHOW℠ program, sponsored by Qualcomm, will highlight the role of STEM in the arts, entertainment, and is empowering young people to create a future full of opportunities and imagination.

Awards and Benefits

Awards: Inspire Award, Think Award, Connect Award, Innovate Award, Design Award, Motivate Award, etc.

Exemplary members from the team that wins the Inspire Award will be fully sponsored for the kit, airfare, and participation costs, food and accommodation to participate in the FIRST Tech Challenge World Championship in the US in April 2024. At the same time, they will be supported by the Vietnamese team and mentors at Maker Vietnam.

FIRST participants can apply for scholarships to colleges, universities, and engineering programs around the world worth over $80 million.

Entry fee is 5,000,000 VND (equivalent to $210 USD). Teams will be contacted for payment details within 2 weeks after filling out the registration form.
